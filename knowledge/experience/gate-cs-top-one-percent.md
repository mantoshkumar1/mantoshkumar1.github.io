# GATE CS & IT Top-1% Achievement and TUM Admission Context

## Result

Mantosh Kumar ranked in the top 1% nationally in the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering paper for Computer Science and Information Technology (GATE CS & IT) in both 2012 and 2013.

## Why the result is notable

The official GATE organization describes GATE as a prestigious national-level examination that assesses comprehensive understanding of undergraduate-level subjects. The examination is conducted by the Indian Institute of Science and the Indian Institutes of Technology on behalf of the National Coordination Board, Department of Higher Education, Ministry of Education, Government of India. GATE results are used for admission to postgraduate programs, possible financial assistance, and some public-sector recruitment.

Official exam context: [Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering](https://gate2026.iitg.ac.in/)

Finishing in the top 1% means Mantosh's result placed him within the highest-performing one percent of candidates in that national CS & IT examination cohort. This is a percentile statement, not a claim about an exact score or all-time rank.

## Technical University of Munich context

Mantosh used a GATE result as part of his admission journey to the M.Sc. in Computer Science at the Technical University of Munich. The published résumé documents the completed TUM degree and thesis work in software-defined networking and network-functions virtualization.

The GATE result is not presented as the sole admission criterion or as proof of a particular historical TUM admissions policy. It is the applicant's factual account of how the result supported his application.

## Evidence boundary

The top-1% results for 2012 and 2013 and the completed TUM degree are documented in Mantosh's published résumé. The connection between the GATE result and his TUM application is Mantosh's firsthand account. No exact GATE score, examination rank, scholarship, or sole-cause admission claim is made.
